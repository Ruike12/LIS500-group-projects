Welcome! This website is a part of Project #2 - Developing Projects Website, where we explore the themes of intersectionality, implicit bias, and technology’s role in shaping social experiences. Our project builds on materials from Project #1, expanding them into a multi-page interactive website.

Project #1 Overview

In our first project, we engaged in critical thinking and discussions about intersectionality and implicit bias through a structured learning process:

Step 1: Exploring Intersectionality & Bias

  • Take an Implicit Bias Test (IBT) – We explored Harvard’s IAT (Implicit Association Test) to reflect on unconscious biases.
  • Alternative Approach – Instead of the test, we could engage with academic discussions, such as reading Robin DiAngelo’s work on racism discourse or Ben Shapiro’s critique of intersectionality.
>

Step 2: Group Video Discussion

In teams of 2-3, we created a 15-minute video discussing:

  • ✔️ Our backgrounds, interests, and reasons for taking this class.
  • ✔️ Our thoughts on bias tests or the readings by DiAngelo and Shapiro.
  • ✔️ How we perceive intersectionality and its role in shaping digital experiences.

Bonus: We explored creative ways to enhance our video with visual elements, accessibility features, and editing effects.

Step 3 & 4: Community Engagement

After posting our video to the Discussion Room & Classroom Café, we engaged with our peers by watching and commenting on two other videos.
