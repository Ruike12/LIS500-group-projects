Implicit Bias Test (IBT) - Harvard IAT

The Harvard Implicit Association Test (IAT) is a widely used tool for uncovering unconscious biases related to race, gender, age, and more. Understanding these biases can help us become more aware of our assumptions and their impact.

Try it yourself: Take the Test

Understanding Intersectionality

Intersectionality describes how overlapping social identities, like race, gender, and class, impact experiences of oppression or privilege.

Watch this short video: Kimberlé Crenshaw, who coined the term "Intersectionality," explains its importance in social justice.

Implicit Bias in Hiring and the Workplace

Many companies claim to support diversity, yet implicit bias in hiring, promotion, and workplace culture creates invisible barriers for marginalized groups.

Read: Tackling Bias in Hiring – McKinsey Report

The Role of AI in Bias

Artificial Intelligence is often seen as neutral, but AI systems inherit human biases because they are trained on existing data.
